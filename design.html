ME 1011 Practice of Mechanical Engineering
Formula Air -- Propeller-Powered Racing Vehicles

Objective:
Design, test, analyze, and build a vehicle with the required functions:
- Autonomous (self-driven)
- Driven forward by the air induced by the rotating propeller(s) only
- Able to trace the black lines on the floor, cross bumpers and dodge barriers (track map as shown in the top right figure)
- Follow another vehicle

In this 5 people group project, my job was basically everything related to the fluids, including the design (Solidworks), numerical analysis (ANSYS Fluent), manufacture (3D printed), thrust test (wind tunnel experiment) of the propeller, the simulations, and drag coefficient measurements of the whole vehicle.

Tools Used

SolidWorks
3D printing (MEX)
ANSYS Fluent
Arduino

ME 3004 Machine Design Theory
Cable Climbing Machine

Objective:
Design, test, and build a remote controlled machine capable of:
- Moving freely on the ground
- Expand vertically to grip on the cable
- Crossing a valley through climbing the cable

Proposed solution:
2 back wheels were driven by 2 AC motors under the chassis respectively, and 1 universal wheel was also attached to the chassis for balancing. A laser-cut scissor-lift structure was applied to extend or shorten in height, and it was pushed by a servo motor with a leadscrew to convert the rotation force into linear motion. Another servo motor on the top with a 3D printed concave wheel was used for climbing the rope.

The figure below shows the stress analysis profile generated from Autodesk Inventor Professional 2018. It successfully predicted the component (a thin steel rod) with the most stresses. The result was proven by a large deformation that occurred on the rod after several test runs, so we later strengthen the structure with PVC tubes.

Tools Used

Autodesk Inventor
3D printing (MEX)
Laser cutting
Arduino

ME 5248 Computer-aided Engineering Drawing
Vernier Caliper

Features:
- With a single click, a 3D vernier caliper can be drawn automatically in seconds.
- The drawing code is written in AutoLISP (the programming language for AutoCAD)
- Precise measurements (virtually in the AutoCAD interface)
