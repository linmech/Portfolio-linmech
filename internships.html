My previous internship experience.

Module reliability team @ Google LLC

Project 1: Reliability study of 5G antenna module inside a cell phone
In this project, I discussed with the team to identify the cause of a specific crack failure occurred on the module in phone drop/tumble tests. To replicate the failure mode, I created a test plan for a shock test and designed the shock jig for the module. The objectives of the shock jig were A. replicate the surrounding conditions of the antenna module in the assembly. B. able to be mounted onto every testing side of the shock table. I designed the shock jig using 3D CAD drawing software NX, and it was my first time using this tool to draw. I only had about two weeks to finish the CAD while gathering every piece of information I need (such as the configuration of the shock table, assembly CAD file of the cellphone) at the same time, so I skipped the tutorials and learned by doing. Also, I had take the manufacturability of the jig into consideration since the aluminum jig would be further manufactured by CNC machining. As a result, I finished the design in one week, and the jig was successfully inplemented in the shock test. Due to the covid situation, I could not retrieve all the test data in time, so I summarized the results I had in a report and shared with the team for investigating the failure boundaries (max. strain before failure) of the module in further study.

Server ME team @ Dell EMC
Design To Cost (DTC) – Server Motherboard Support Solution Optimization

Those rubber foot pads are glued to the bottom of the server’s motherboard to reduce strain. In several models, they were designed in different geometries, leading to different costs.

My task was to find out an optimized design solution, since this rubber foot pad feature will be retained in the next generation. If the design is standardized, the overall cost could be lowered.

After consolidating current rubber support applications in different server models, I generalized several design variables to evaluate in the later experiement I performed.

To evaluate the importance of each design factor, I conducted a system-level test on a vibration table. By measuring the transmissibility ratio (tr, the ratio of the acceleration on MB to the acceleration on vibration table) on the surface of the motherboard in the range of the system’s first resonant frequency, the energy reduction performances of the rubber foot pads can thus be determined.
